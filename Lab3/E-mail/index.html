Despite their short legs, corgis are energetic dogs that require regular exercise to maintain a healthy weight and prevent boredom. They enjoy walks, playtime, and interactive toys. Training corgis can be a joy due to their intelligence and eagerness to please. Early socialization and consistent, positive reinforcement training are important to ensure they grow into well-behaved adult dogs. Corgis are generally healthy dogs, but like all breeds, they are prone to certain health issues such as hip dysplasia, degenerative myelopathy, and eye conditions.
